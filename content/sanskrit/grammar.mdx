# Sanskrit Grammar :icon[Layers]

Sanskrit grammar is orderly. Instead of memorizing every form at once, start by noticing what a word is doing in the sentence.

## Nouns

Nouns change form based on role, number, and gender.

:::info
The role of a noun is often visible in its ending, so word order can be more flexible than in English.
:::

## Verbs

Verbs carry information about person, number, tense, and mood.

| Sanskrit | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| पठति | he or she reads |
| पठतः | two read |
| पठन्ति | they read |

## Agreement

Adjectives usually agree with the nouns they describe. Reveal the key idea: adjectives follow the noun in [[gender, number, and case]].

:::warning
Do not rush declension tables. Learn a few useful forms in real sentences first.
:::
