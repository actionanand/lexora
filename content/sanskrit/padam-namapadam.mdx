Sanskrit reading becomes easier when you know the difference between a **word by itself** and a **word ready for a sentence**.

## Shabdah And Padam

In Sanskrit, **शब्दः** (*śabdaḥ*) is a broad word. Its first sense is **sound**. It can mean any audible sound, such as the ringing of a bell or the chirping of birds. In grammar, it can also mean a meaningful linguistic expression, or a word.

**शब्दाः** (*śabdāḥ*) means **sounds / words**. A **शब्दः** may or may not be ready to stand inside a sentence.

**पदम्** (*padam*) is a technical grammar word. It means a complete inflected word that can stand independently in a sentence.

:::info[Pāṇini's Rule]
**सुप्तिङन्तं पदम्** (suptiṅantaṃ padam) means a form ending in a nominal case ending (**सुप्**) or a verbal ending (**तिङ्**) is called **पदम्**.
:::

| Term | Transliteration | Simple Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| :tableCell[शब्दः]{sub="śabdaḥ"} | :tableCell[sound / word]{sub="can be broad"} | :tableCell[घंटायाः शब्दः]{sub="the sound of a bell"} |
| :tableCell[शब्दाः]{sub="śabdāḥ"} | :tableCell[sounds / words]{sub="plural"} | :tableCell[पक्षिणां शब्दः]{sub="the sound of birds"} |
| :tableCell[पदम्]{sub="padam"} | :tableCell[inflected sentence word]{sub="complete usable form"} | :tableCell[रामः, गच्छति]{sub="both are पदानि"} |

For example, **रामः** and **गच्छति** are **पदानि** because they can stand in a sentence. But **राम** as a stem and **गम्** as a root are **शब्दाः**, not complete **पदानि**.

:::tip
Every **पदम्** is also a **शब्दः**, but not every **शब्दः** is a **पदम्**.
:::

## Namapadam

**नामपदम्** (*nāmapadam*) means **noun** in English and **பெயர்ச்சொல்** in Tamil. It is a naming word. It names a person, animal, object, place, or idea.

From the lesson chart:

| Group | Pattern | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| :tableCell[अकारान्तः पुल्लिङ्गशब्दः]{sub="akārāntaḥ puṃliṅgaśabdaḥ"} | :tableCell[a-ending masculine]{sub="ஆண்பால் பெயர்ச்சொல்"} | :tableCell[बालकः, वानरः, सिंहः, गजः, अश्वः]{sub="boy, monkey, lion, elephant, horse"} |
| :tableCell[अकारान्तः नपुंसकलिङ्गशब्दः]{sub="akārāntaḥ napuṃsakaliṅgaśabdaḥ"} | :tableCell[a-ending neuter]{sub="அஃறிணை பெயர்ச்சொல்"} | :tableCell[फलम्, विमानम्, दुग्धम्, पुष्पम्, जलम्]{sub="fruit, airplane, milk, flower, water"} |
| :tableCell[आकारान्तः स्त्रीलिङ्गशब्दः]{sub="ākārāntaḥ strīliṅgaśabdaḥ"} | :tableCell[ā-ending feminine]{sub="பெண்பால் பெயர்ச்சொல்"} | :tableCell[बालिका, शाटिका, पेटिका, लता, देवता]{sub="girl, sari, box, creeper, deity"} |
| :tableCell[ईकारान्तः स्त्रीलिङ्गशब्दः]{sub="īkārāntaḥ strīliṅgaśabdaḥ"} | :tableCell[ī-ending feminine]{sub="பெண்பால் பெயர்ச்சொல்"} | :tableCell[जननी, भगिनी, लेखनी, घटी, सरस्वती]{sub="mother, sister, pen, pot, Sarasvati"} |

:::tip[Reading Tip]
The ending helps you guess the gender pattern, but always learn the word with its actual usage. Some words need memory, not only a rule.
:::

## Sarvanamapadam

**सर्वनामपदम्** (*sarvanāmapadam*) is a **pronoun word**. In Tamil, it is close to **சுட்டுப்பெயர்** or a word that points to a noun. It points to a noun or replaces a noun.

For beginners, learn these pairs first:

| ==Near== object | Gender | ==Far== object |
| --- | --- | --- |
| :tableCell[एषः]{sub="eṣaḥ"} | masculine | :tableCell[सः]{sub="saḥ"} |
| :tableCell[एषा]{sub="eṣā"} | feminine | :tableCell[सा]{sub="sā"} |
| :tableCell[एतत्]{sub="etat"} | neuter | :tableCell[तत्]{sub="tat"} |

The pronoun must match the gender of the noun.

| Noun | Gender | ==Near== sentence | ==Far== sentence |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| बालकः | masculine | ==एषः== बालकः | ==सः== बालकः |
| बालिका | feminine | ==एषा== बालिका | ==सा== बालिका |
| फलम् | neuter | ==एतत्== फलम् | ==तत्== फलम् |

## Quick Practice

1. Say the noun: **बालकः**.
2. Add the near pronoun: **एषः बालकः**.
3. Add the far pronoun: **सः बालकः**.
4. Repeat with **बालिका** and **फलम्**.
